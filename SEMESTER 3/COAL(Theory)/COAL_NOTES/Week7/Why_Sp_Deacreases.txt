# Why Does SP Decrease on PUSH?

 In 8086 assembly, the **stack grows downward** in memory. This means the top of the stack (where `SP` points) moves to *lower* addresses each time you push something.

### Visualizing Stack Growth
Suppose the stack starts at a higher memory address (say, `2000h`).

- When you `PUSH` a word:
    - `SP` gets **decreased by 2** (because 8086 stack works with 16-bit words)
    - The value is stored at the new `SS:SP` address

**Example:**

If `SP = 2000h`, and you `PUSH AX`, then:

2. The value of `AX` is stored at memory `[SS:1FFEh]`
