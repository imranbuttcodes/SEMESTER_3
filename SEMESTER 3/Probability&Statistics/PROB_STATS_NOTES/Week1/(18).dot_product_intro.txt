## ðŸ”¹ Definition of Dot Product

For two vectors in (\mathbb{R}^n):
[
\vec{u} = \langle u_1, u_2, \dots, u_n \rangle, \quad
\vec{v} = \langle v_1, v_2, \dots, v_n \rangle
]

The **dot product** is defined as:
[
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_1v_1 + u_2v_2 + \cdots + u_nv_n
]

---

## ðŸ”¹ Alternative (Geometric) Definition

The dot product also relates to the angle (\theta) between the two vectors:
[
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = |\vec{u}| , |\vec{v}| \cos\theta
]

where

* (|\vec{u}|) = magnitude of (\vec{u})
* (|\vec{v}|) = magnitude of (\vec{v})
* (\theta) = angle between (\vec{u}) and (\vec{v})

---

## ðŸ”¹ Why do we need it?

1. To find the **angle between two vectors**.
2. To check if two vectors are **orthogonal (perpendicular)**:
   [
   \vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = 0 \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \vec{u} \perp \vec{v}
   ]
3. To project one vector onto another.
4. Very useful in **physics** (work = force Â· displacement).
