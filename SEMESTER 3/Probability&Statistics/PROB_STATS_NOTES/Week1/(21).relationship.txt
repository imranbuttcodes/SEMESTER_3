---

### 1. Length of the third side

The vector (\vec{u} - \vec{v}) has magnitude:
[
|\vec{u} - \vec{v}|
]

So in the triangle, the sides are:

* one side = (|\vec{u}|)
* one side = (|\vec{v}|)
* one side = (|\vec{u} - \vec{v}|)

and the angle between (\vec{u}) and (\vec{v}) is (\theta).

---

### 2. Apply Law of Cosines

Law of cosines says:
[
|\vec{u} - \vec{v}|^2 = |\vec{u}|^2 + |\vec{v}|^2 - 2|\vec{u}||\vec{v}|\cos\theta
]

---

### 3. Expand using vector algebra

On the other hand,
[
|\vec{u} - \vec{v}|^2 = (\vec{u} - \vec{v}) \cdot (\vec{u} - \vec{v})
]

Expand:
[
= \vec{u}\cdot\vec{u} - 2\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v} + \vec{v}\cdot\vec{v}
]

[
= |\vec{u}|^2 + |\vec{v}|^2 - 2(\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v})
]

---

### 4. Compare both equations

We have:
[
|\vec{u}|^2 + |\vec{v}|^2 - 2(\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}) ;=; |\vec{u}|^2 + |\vec{v}|^2 - 2|\vec{u}||\vec{v}|\cos\theta
]

Cancel terms:
[
-2(\vec{u}\cdot\vec{v}) = -2|\vec{u}||\vec{v}|\cos\theta
]

[
\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = |\vec{u}| , |\vec{v}| \cos\theta
